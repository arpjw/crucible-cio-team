# Fundraising — Document Index

> **Regulatory notice:** If your fund is registered as an investment adviser or your manager is operating under CPO Exemption 4.13(a)(3), general solicitation is prohibited. Exemption 4.13(a)(3) explicitly prohibits marketing the fund through general solicitation or advertising. SEC Rule 506(b) under Regulation D also prohibits general solicitation. These materials are for use **only with pre-existing substantive relationships** and **only with persons who qualify as accredited investors or qualified eligible persons** before any investment discussion. "Pre-existing substantive relationship" means you know the investor well enough to assess their financial sophistication before any conversation about the fund. Meeting someone at a conference does not create a pre-existing relationship. Consult fund counsel before distributing any marketing materials. If you intend to generally solicit, you must use Rule 506(c) and verify accredited investor status through affirmative means — and you cannot use 4.13(a)(3). These are separate legal frameworks and you cannot mix them.

## Fundraising Principles for a Sub-$10M Systematic Fund

**1. Your track record is your product.** Before any LP meeting, have at minimum 6 months of live track record and preferably 12 months. LPs who invest at month three are betting on you as a person, not a strategy. That is a different (and smaller) market.

**2. Process documentation beats performance claims.** Any LP who has done this before has seen hundreds of managers claim a Sharpe ratio over 1.5. What differentiates you at this size is the specificity and credibility of your process documentation: signal validation, backtest methodology, risk limits, drawdown protocols. The DDQ is your process audit.

**3. Match the LP to the stage.** At sub-$10M, you are not a target for most endowments, foundations, or large family offices. You are a target for (a) friends and family who trust you personally, (b) high-net-worth individuals with a track record of backing emerging managers, and (c) specific family offices with an explicit emerging manager mandate. Do not waste months pursuing institutional LPs who have $50M minimums before you have $25M AUM.

**4. Rejection is not failure — it is data.** Most investors at the emerging manager stage pass for reasons unrelated to fund quality: they are already at capacity for the strategy, they have a moratorium on new managers, their team changed. Get the specific reason. If you hear the same reason from three different LPs, it is real feedback.

**5. Prepare the DDQ before you need it.** An institutional LP who expresses interest will ask for a DDQ within 48 hours. If you cannot produce it, you are eliminated. Complete the [DDQ template](../legal/templates/ddq-template.md) before your first meeting.

**6. Manage your pipeline explicitly.** Use a CRM (even a spreadsheet) to track every LP contact: introduction date, last touchpoint, fund materials sent, outstanding questions, expected decision timeline. Fundraising at sub-$50M is a 6–18 month process. You will forget conversations without records.

## Fundraising Document Workflow

```
LP targeting guide → identify and prioritize your LP universe
       ↓
Pitch deck → first meeting / warm introduction (no materials until pre-existing relationship exists)
       ↓
DDQ → LP requests due diligence package (have this ready before any meeting)
       ↓
Subscription documents → LP commits (OM + subscription agreement from fund counsel)
       ↓
LP letter → monthly reporting to all investors once capital is accepted
```

## Cost Estimates for Fundraising Support

| Item | Estimated Cost |
|---|---|
| Pitch deck design (professional) | $1,500–$5,000 |
| Capital introduction (placement agent) | 2–5% of capital raised (varies significantly) |
| Compliance review of marketing materials | $2,000–$5,000 (one-time review by fund counsel) |
| CRM software (HubSpot, Affinity, etc.) | $0–$500/month |
| Conference attendance (iConnections, Context Summits, etc.) | $2,000–$10,000 per conference |

Note on placement agents: At sub-$10M AUM, most institutional placement agents will not work with you — their minimum commitment size makes it uneconomical. At $10–$25M AUM, some boutique placement agents will engage on a best-efforts basis. Verify FINRA registration (Form BD) before engaging any placement agent.
