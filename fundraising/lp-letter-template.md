# Monthly LP Letter — Three Variants

> **Compliance note:** LP letters are marketing communications subject to the adviser advertising rules. Do not include performance claims without required context. Do not use language that implies a guarantee of future results. Have counsel review your first letter and any letter sent after a significant drawdown. Send all letters to all LPs simultaneously — selective disclosure creates fairness and potentially Reg FD-adjacent issues even in private fund contexts.

---

## Variant B — Drawdown Month

*Use when: Monthly performance was materially negative (-3% or worse), approaching drawdown limit, or when the drawdown is large enough that LPs will have concerns about the strategy functioning as described.*

**Tone guidance:** Direct, factual, and calm. Do not minimize a real loss — LPs will lose trust. Do not catastrophize or express panic — that signals loss of emotional control, which is disqualifying. The right tone is: "here is what happened, here is our analysis, here is what we are doing." The worst thing you can do in a drawdown letter is be vague about what caused the loss.

---

**[FUND LEGAL NAME]**
**Monthly Investor Letter**
**[MONTH] [YEAR]**

---

Dear [LP NAME / "Partners"],

[FUND NAME] returned **[NET RETURN %]** net of fees in [MONTH]. Year-to-date, the fund has returned **[YTD NET RETURN %]**. The fund's drawdown from its high-water mark stands at **[DRAWDOWN %]**.

We want to provide you with a transparent account of what drove performance this month and where the portfolio stands.

**Performance Summary**

| Period | Net Return |
|---|---|
| [Month] [Year] | [X.X%] |
| Year-to-date | [X.X%] |
| Since inception ([MONTH YEAR]) | [X.X%] |
| Current drawdown from HWM | [X.X%] |
| Maximum drawdown since inception | [X.X%] |

Current AUM: $[X.XM]

**What Happened**

[Clear, specific explanation of what drove the loss. Avoid jargon. This section should answer: what positions lost money, why those positions lost money, whether this was a market condition the strategy is known to perform poorly in, or whether something unexpected occurred. 3–5 sentences.]

Example: "The loss in [MONTH] was driven primarily by [MARKET/POSITION], which moved adversely by [X%] against our positioning. This move was [EXPECTED IN THIS ENVIRONMENT / ATYPICAL — specify which]. Our [DIRECTION] position was sized at [X%] of NAV, consistent with our stated position limits. Total realized loss on the position was [X%] of NAV."

**Context: Is This Within the Expected Range of Outcomes?**

[1–3 sentences that put the loss in context of the strategy's historical volatility and your disclosed risk parameters. Do not rationalize a strategy failure as normal volatility if it was not. Be honest.]

Example: "Monthly losses of this magnitude are within the expected range given the fund's annualized volatility target of [X%]. Since inception, the fund has experienced [N] months with losses exceeding [Y%]. The maximum historical monthly loss prior to this month was [Z%] in [MONTH YEAR]."

OR (if this is abnormal):
"This month's loss exceeded our typical monthly loss range. We are conducting a post-mortem analysis of whether this represents a signal failure, a position sizing error, or a genuine regime change. We will update you with our findings."

**Portfolio Positioning and Risk Status**

[2–3 sentences on where the portfolio stands today. Current drawdown vs. your halt threshold. Any position changes made as a result of the drawdown. Current gross/net exposure.]

Example: "Following this month's loss, the fund is at [X%] drawdown from its high-water mark. Our drawdown protocol establishes a review at [Y%] drawdown and a trading halt at [Z%] drawdown. The portfolio continues to operate normally within its stated parameters / [OR: We have initiated our drawdown review protocol and have [DESCRIBE ACTION].]"

**Our Commitment**

[2–4 sentences, personal tone. Acknowledge the loss plainly. State what you are doing to analyze it. State your confidence in the strategy or your concerns honestly — LPs do not want to be managed, they want to be informed.]

Example: "We take this month's performance seriously. Every significant loss is an opportunity to examine whether our process is working as designed or whether changes are warranted. Our current assessment is [HONEST STATEMENT]. We are committed to managing your capital with the same discipline described in our offering documents and to communicating transparently when outcomes do not match expectations."

**Redemption Process**

[Optional, include only if the drawdown is large enough that LPs may want to redeem.] "As a reminder, redemption requests for the [QUARTER-END] redemption date must be received by [DATE]. Please contact us at [EMAIL] if you wish to discuss your options."

[EMAIL] | [PHONE]

## Variant C — First Letter After Launch

*Use when: Sending the very first letter to investors after the fund has been trading for its first month or first quarter.*

**Purpose:** This letter has three jobs beyond the monthly update: (1) formally welcome investors into the fund and confirm they are enrolled correctly, (2) set expectations for what you will report and how often, and (3) introduce yourself as a communicator — LPs will judge the quality and clarity of your first letter against every future letter.

## Mandatory Disclosures — What Must Be Disclosed Regardless of Performance

The following information must be disclosed in your LP letter program regardless of whether it is convenient or reflects well on the fund:

**1. Accurate performance.** Do not round up or present best-day estimates. Net returns should reflect actual NAV calculations from your fund administrator.

**2. Drawdown depth.** If the fund is in a drawdown from its high-water mark, state the drawdown percentage. Do not omit this because it is painful.

**3. Any change in strategy or process.** If you modified signal parameters, changed risk limits, added a new instrument class, or changed execution infrastructure, disclose it. LPs invested based on the strategy described in your offering documents — material changes require disclosure and may require LP consent under the LPA.

**4. Any change in service providers.** Prime broker changes, administrator changes, auditor changes — disclose in the letter for the month in which the change occurred.

**5. Any regulatory event.** If you receive a regulatory inquiry, examination notice, or enforcement action, consult counsel immediately and disclose as required. Do not bury regulatory disclosures in footnotes.

**6. Any material operational incident.** Technology failures that affected trading, data errors that affected NAV, execution errors that caused material losses — disclose and explain remediation.

**7. Key person departure.** If a key person (as defined in your fund documents) departs, this typically triggers a required LP notification under the LPA. Check your documents and consult counsel.

**8. Suspension of redemptions or gate invocation.** If you invoke a gate provision or suspend redemptions, notify all LPs immediately in writing, not in the monthly letter. The monthly letter should then reference the event and its status.
