# Series 65 Exam — 8-Week Study Plan

> **What is the Series 65?** The Uniform Investment Adviser Law Examination (Series 65) is administered by NASAA through FINRA. Passing it qualifies you to register as an Investment Adviser Representative (IAR) in most U.S. states. It is the primary exam required for individuals providing investment advice for compensation, including fund managers registering as investment advisers. No sponsoring broker-dealer is required.

**Exam format:** 130 questions (10 unscored pretest questions) | 180-minute time limit | 72% passing score (94 correct of 130 scored)
**Exam fee:** $187 (paid to FINRA at registration)
**Scheduling:** Prometric test centers; online proctored option available
**Registration:** finra.org/registration-exams-ce/qualification-exams/series65

---

## Four Topic Areas and Weights

| Topic Area | Weight | # of Questions (approx.) |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Economic Factors and Business Information | 15% | 19–20 |
| 2. Investment Vehicle Characteristics | 25% | 33–34 |
| 3. Client Investment Recommendations and Strategies | 30% | 39–40 |
| 4. Laws, Regulations, and Guidelines | 30% | 39–40 |

**Where to focus:** Topics 3 and 4 are each 30% and together determine whether you pass or fail. Do not underinvest in regulation — it is more learnable than it looks.

## Recommended Study Materials

**Primary:**
- **Kaplan Financial Education** (kaplanfinancial.com): Industry standard. Complete package includes textbook, online question bank (3,000+ questions), video lectures, and practice exams. Cost: $200–$350.
- **Securities Institute of America** (securitiesce.com): Strong alternative. Known for clear explanations of regulatory topics. Cost: $150–$250.

**Supplemental:**
- **Investopedia Series 65 prep:** Free articles on specific concepts (good for quick reinforcement, not primary prep)
- **Pass Perfect** (passperfect.com): Question bank focused. Good for drill practice in final two weeks.
- **NASAA Content Outline:** Free at nasaa.org. This is the official blueprint. Print it and track your coverage against it weekly.

**Daily time commitment:** 1.5 hours/day, 6 days/week = 9 hours/week = 72 hours over 8 weeks. This is sufficient for most candidates with a finance background. Candidates without a finance background should plan 90 hours minimum.

## Week-by-Week Breakdown

### Week 1 — Economic Factors and Business Information (Topic 1, 15%)

**Goal:** Build foundational vocabulary and economic framework. This is the lightest topic by weight but the most useful context for the rest of the exam.

**Topics to cover:**
- Business cycles: expansion, peak, contraction, trough; leading vs. lagging indicators
- Monetary policy: Fed tools (open market operations, discount rate, reserve requirements), quantitative easing, yield curve implications
- Fiscal policy: deficit spending, automatic stabilizers, Keynesian vs. supply-side
- Inflation types: demand-pull vs. cost-push; CPI, PPI, PCE measurement
- Balance of payments: current account, capital account, exchange rate regimes
- Financial statements: income statement, balance sheet, cash flow statement; basic ratios (P/E, P/B, debt/equity, current ratio)
- Corporate actions: stock splits, dividends (ex-date, record date, payment date), rights offerings, spin-offs

**Daily plan:** 90 minutes. Read one chapter, do 30–40 practice questions on that chapter. Don't skip the questions — passive reading does not build exam reflexes.

**End of week target:** 70%+ on Topic 1 practice questions.

---

### Week 2 — Investment Vehicle Characteristics, Part 1 (Topic 2, 25%)

**Goal:** Cover equity securities, debt securities, and packaged products.

**Topics to cover:**
- Common stock vs. preferred stock: rights, dividends, liquidation priority
- Corporate bonds: coupon, par, yield to maturity, yield to call, duration, convexity
- Government securities: T-bills, T-notes, T-bonds, TIPS, agency bonds
- Municipal bonds: GO bonds vs. revenue bonds, tax treatment, equivalent taxable yield
- Mortgage-backed securities: pass-throughs, CMOs, prepayment risk
- Mutual funds: open-end vs. closed-end, NAV calculation, share classes, 12b-1 fees
- ETFs vs. mutual funds: creation/redemption mechanism, premium/discount, tax efficiency
- Variable annuities and variable life insurance: accumulation units, annuity units, riders, suitability concerns

**Daily plan:** 90 minutes. Read and question bank. Focus on yield calculations — the exam tests your ability to calculate, not just identify.

**End of week target:** 70%+ on equity and bond questions.

---

### Week 3 — Investment Vehicle Characteristics, Part 2 + First Practice Exam

**Goal:** Complete Topic 2, identify weak spots, take first full practice exam.

**Topics to cover:**
- Options: calls, puts, long vs. short, breakeven calculations, max gain/loss
- Futures and forwards: mechanics, hedging vs. speculation, basis risk
- Alternative investments: hedge fund structures, limited partnerships, REITs (equity vs. mortgage), 1031 exchanges
- Real assets: direct real estate, commodities, infrastructure
- 529 plans, Coverdell ESAs, UGMA/UTMA: contribution limits, qualified expenses, control

**Practice exam (Saturday or Sunday):** Take one full 130-question practice exam under timed conditions. Do not study the questions before taking it — treat it as a diagnostic. Score it. Review every incorrect answer.

**End of week target:** First practice exam score ≥ 60%. (Below 60% — add 30 min/day next week.)

---

### Week 4 — Client Investment Recommendations and Strategies, Part 1 (Topic 3, 30%)

**Goal:** Cover portfolio theory, asset allocation, and client profiling.

**Topics to cover:**
- Modern Portfolio Theory: efficient frontier, Capital Market Line, Capital Asset Pricing Model, beta, alpha, Sharpe ratio, Treynor ratio
- Asset allocation: strategic vs. tactical, rebalancing triggers, correlation and diversification
- Risk types: systematic vs. unsystematic, market, interest rate, credit, liquidity, currency, inflation, legislative
- Client profiling: risk tolerance assessment, time horizon, liquidity needs, tax situation, investment objectives (growth, income, capital preservation)
- Suitability standard: know-your-customer requirements, documenting suitability
- Fiduciary standard vs. suitability standard: when each applies, how they differ

**Daily plan:** 90 minutes. Memorize the CAPM formula. Know how to calculate portfolio beta given component betas and weights. Know Sharpe, Treynor, and Jensen's alpha formulas.

**End of week target:** 72%+ on portfolio theory and asset allocation questions.

---

### Week 5 — Client Investment Recommendations and Strategies, Part 2

**Goal:** Complete Topic 3, covering retirement accounts and specific strategy types.

**Topics to cover:**
- Retirement accounts: Traditional IRA, Roth IRA (income limits, contribution limits, conversion rules), 401(k), 403(b), SEP-IRA, SIMPLE IRA, defined benefit vs. defined contribution
- Required Minimum Distributions: age triggers, calculation method, exceptions
- Tax strategies: tax-loss harvesting, asset location (tax-efficient vs. tax-inefficient assets in taxable vs. tax-deferred accounts), step-up in basis
- Income needs: systematic withdrawal plans, annuitization, reverse dollar cost averaging
- Education planning: 529 vs. Coverdell vs. UGMA/UTMA trade-offs
- Business succession: buy-sell agreements, key person insurance, deferred compensation

**Second practice exam (Saturday or Sunday):** Second full 130-question timed exam. Compare score to Week 3.

**End of week target:** Second practice exam score ≥ 65%. Topics 1 and 2 should be stable — any regression needs attention.

---

### Week 6 — Laws, Regulations, and Guidelines, Part 1 (Topic 4, 30%)

**Goal:** Cover the Investment Advisers Act of 1940 and state IA regulation.

**This is the most memorization-intensive topic. Do not rush it.**

**Topics to cover:**
- Investment Advisers Act of 1940: definition of investment adviser, exclusions (broker-dealer exclusion, publisher exclusion, accountant/attorney/engineer exclusions), registration requirements
- Form ADV: Part 1 (organizational and disciplinary info, filed on IARD), Part 2A (brochure — narrative disclosure), Part 2B (brochure supplement — individual adviser info)
- Investment Adviser Representatives (IARs): who must register, where, how
- Federal vs. state registration: federal covered advisers (SEC, $110M+ AUM), state advisers (under $100M AUM), buffer zone ($100–$110M)
- Brochure rule: delivery requirements (upon entering contract, annually if material changes, upon request)
- Fiduciary duty components: duty of loyalty, duty of care; principal transactions; agency cross transactions
- Prohibited practices: cherry-picking, performance guarantees, misleading advertising, scalping (recommending securities while planning to sell own position)
- Custody rules: Rule 206(4)-2; surprise audit requirements; qualified custodians

**Daily plan:** 90 minutes. For regulatory topics, use flashcards heavily — the exam loves testing specific numerical thresholds and time deadlines.

**Key numbers to memorize:**
- $110M: SEC registration threshold (mandatory for most advisers)
- $100M: state registration ceiling
- $25M–$100M: state-registered (with exemptions down to $0 in some states)
- 48 hours: advance brochure delivery requirement before entering advisory contract
- 5 days: brochure delivery on or before signing if client waives 48-hour advance delivery? No — still must deliver before signing
- 120 days after fiscal year end: Form ADV annual updating amendment deadline

**End of week target:** 70%+ on IA regulatory questions.

---

### Week 7 — Laws, Regulations, and Guidelines, Part 2

**Goal:** Complete Topic 4, covering securities laws, anti-fraud provisions, and ethics.

**Topics to cover:**
- Securities Act of 1933: registration of new securities, prospectus requirements, exemptions (Reg D 506(b) and 506(c), Reg A, Rule 144, Rule 144A)
- Securities Exchange Act of 1934: registration of exchanges, broker-dealers, reporting companies; Section 10(b) and Rule 10b-5 anti-fraud; insider trading prohibition
- Investment Company Act of 1940: registered investment company types (open-end, closed-end, UITs), exemptions (3(c)(1) for 100 investors, 3(c)(7) for QEPs)
- Uniform Securities Act (USA): state securities registration, agent and broker-dealer registration, exempt transactions, anti-fraud provisions
- NASAA's Model Rules: ethical standards for investment advisers, prohibited conduct
- ERISA basics: fiduciary standards for pension plan advisers, prohibited transactions, plan types

**Third practice exam (Saturday or Sunday):** Third full timed exam. This is the benchmark exam. If you are scoring 72%+, you are ready to test. If 65–72%, one more week. If below 65%, reassess and add time.

**End of week target:** Third practice exam score ≥ 72%.

---

### Week 8 — Review, Weak Spot Drilling, and Go/No-Go Decision

**Goal:** Consolidate, not learn new material. Drill weak areas. Make the scheduling decision.

**Daily plan:**
- Days 1–2: Review all incorrect answers from practice exams 1–3. Make a list of every topic where you missed >30% of questions.
- Days 3–4: Do 50–100 questions per day exclusively in weak topic areas. Use a fresh question bank if available.
- Day 5: Review key formulas and numerical thresholds. Read NASAA Content Outline one final time and confirm coverage.
- Day 6: One final full practice exam under exam conditions. No review immediately after — get sleep.
- Day 7: Rest. Light review only. No new practice exams.

### Go/No-Go Decision Framework

| Condition | Decision |
|---|---|
| Three practice exam average ≥ 72% | Schedule and sit. You are ready. |
| Three practice exam average 68–71% | Schedule and sit. Most candidates score 2–5 points higher on the actual exam due to lower cognitive load. |
| Three practice exam average 65–67% | Delay 1–2 weeks. Drill weak topics. Do not sit below this threshold. |
| Three practice exam average < 65% | Delay 2–3 weeks. Reassess study approach. Consider a live prep course or tutor. |
| Any single topic area below 60% on all three exams | Delay until that topic is above 65%. Passing requires balance across all topics. |

## Exam Day Checklist

- [ ] Bring two valid government-issued IDs (one must have photo)
- [ ] Arrive at test center 30 minutes early
- [ ] No personal items in testing room (phone, wallet, watch — all in locker)
- [ ] Calculator provided at testing center (no personal calculators)
- [ ] 180 minutes; flag uncertain questions and return — do not leave blanks
- [ ] Passing score: 72% (94 of 130 scored questions correct)

## After Passing

1. Receive score notification immediately at test center.
2. Apply for IAR registration in your home state through NASAA IARD system (iard.com).
3. File Form U4 through IARD — requires your CRD (Central Registration Depository) number.
4. Pay state IAR registration fee (typically $30–$200 per state).
5. If also registering the firm as an IA, file Form ADV through IARD simultaneously.
6. Complete any state-specific continuing education requirements (varies by state).
