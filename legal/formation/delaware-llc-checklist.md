# Delaware LLC Formation Checklist — Fund Management Company

> Use this checklist to form the management company entity (not the fund itself). The management company is the LLC that serves as general partner or investment manager. It employs the team, holds the management agreement, and is the entity that registers with regulators.

**Entity type:** Delaware Limited Liability Company
**Typical timeline:** 2–4 weeks end-to-end (expedited: 3–5 business days for state filings)
**Typical all-in cost (state fees only):** $250–$500 | With counsel: $2,000–$8,000

---

## Step 1 — Choose and Verify Entity Name Availability

- [ ] **Status:** Not started / In progress / Complete

**What you need before starting:** A list of 3–5 candidate names in priority order.

**What you do:**
- Search the Delaware Division of Corporations entity name database at corp.delaware.gov. The search is free.
- Name must include "LLC," "L.L.C.," or "Limited Liability Company."
- Name cannot be identical or deceptively similar to an existing Delaware entity.
- Check FINRA BrokerCheck and NFA BASIC for conflicts with registered entities.
- Check USPTO trademark database for conflicts with registered marks.
- Reserve the name if needed (optional, $75 fee, 120-day hold via Delaware Division of Corporations).

**Estimated cost:** $0 search / $75 optional reservation
**Estimated time:** 1–2 hours

**When done, you have:** A confirmed available entity name and optional name reservation.

---

## Step 2 — Appoint a Registered Agent

- [ ] **Status:** Not started / In progress / Complete

**What you need before starting:** Entity name confirmed (Step 1).

**What a registered agent does:** Receives legal documents, government correspondence, and service of process on behalf of your LLC. Required by Delaware law — you cannot serve as your own registered agent unless you have a physical Delaware address.

**What you do:**
- Select a registered agent with a physical Delaware address.
- Sign up and pay annual fee.
- Receive the agent's address (used on Certificate of Formation).

**Recommended vendors (not an endorsement — verify current pricing):**
- CT Corporation / Wolters Kluwer: $260–$350/year, enterprise-grade
- Harvard Business Services: $50/year, popular for small funds
- Incfile / Northwest Registered Agent: $125–$200/year, service varies

**Estimated cost:** $50–$350/year
**Estimated time:** 30 minutes to select and sign up

**When done, you have:** A registered agent address to use on the Certificate of Formation.

---

## Step 3 — File Certificate of Formation

- [ ] **Status:** Not started / In progress / Complete

**What you need before starting:** Entity name (Step 1), registered agent address (Step 2).

**What you do:**
- File the **Certificate of Formation** (official form name) with the Delaware Division of Corporations.
- The Certificate requires: LLC name, registered agent name and address, and an authorized signature.
- File online at corp.delaware.gov or by mail to: Division of Corporations, 401 Federal St., Suite 4, Dover, DE 19901.

**Filing fees:**
- Standard filing fee: **$90**
- 24-hour expedited processing: $100 additional
- Same-day processing: $200–$500 additional (varies by submission time)

**Processing time:**
- Standard (mail): 5–10 business days
- Online standard: 3–5 business days
- 24-hour expedited: next business day
- Same-day: same business day if filed before noon ET

**Estimated cost:** $90–$590 depending on speed
**Estimated time:** 15 minutes to prepare; 1–10 business days for state processing

**When done, you have:** A stamped Certificate of Formation — proof that the LLC legally exists in Delaware.

---

## Step 4 — Obtain EIN from IRS

- [ ] **Status:** Not started / In progress / Complete

**What you need before starting:** Stamped Certificate of Formation (Step 3).

**What you do:**
- File **Form SS-4** (Application for Employer Identification Number) with the IRS.
- The EIN identifies your business for tax purposes (like a Social Security Number for the entity).

**How to file:**
- **Online (recommended):** IRS.gov → "Apply for an EIN Online." EIN issued immediately at the end of the session. Available Monday–Friday 7am–10pm ET. You must have a valid SSN or ITIN to use the online system.
- **Fax:** Fax Form SS-4 to the IRS. EIN issued in 4 business days.
- **Mail:** Mail Form SS-4. EIN issued in 4–6 weeks. Do not do this.

**On the form, select:** "Limited Liability Company" as entity type; number of members; "Investment company" or "Finance and insurance" as the reason for applying.

**Estimated cost:** $0 (IRS does not charge for EINs)
**Estimated time:** 15 minutes online; immediate issuance

**When done, you have:** An EIN confirmation letter (CP 575) — required to open a bank account and for tax filings.

---

## Step 5 — Draft Operating Agreement

- [ ] **Status:** Not started / In progress / Complete

**What you need before starting:** EIN (Step 4), clarity on ownership structure (single-member or multi-member), decision on management structure (member-managed vs. manager-managed).

**What you do:**
- Draft an Operating Agreement governing the internal rules of the LLC.
- Delaware does not require an Operating Agreement to be filed with the state, but it is legally required under Delaware law and essential for bank accounts, investor diligence, and disputes.

**Key provisions to include (see [Operating Agreement Outline](../templates/operating-agreement-outline.md) for detail):**
- **Management structure:** Member-managed (all members vote) vs. manager-managed (designated manager has authority). Most fund management companies are manager-managed with the founder as manager.
- **Capital contributions:** What each member contributes, whether future contributions are required, how new members are admitted.
- **Profit and loss allocation:** Percentage allocations; ensure they match economic intent.
- **Distributions:** When distributions can be made, who approves them, whether there is a priority order.
- **Dissolution:** Vote required to dissolve, how assets are distributed on wind-down.

**Estimated cost:** $0 if self-drafted (not recommended) / $1,500–$5,000 with counsel
**Estimated time:** 1–3 weeks with counsel

**When done, you have:** A signed Operating Agreement — governs the entity and is required for bank account opening and regulatory filings.

## Step 6 — Open Business Bank Account

- [ ] **Status:** Not started / In progress / Complete

**What you need before starting:** Certificate of Formation (Step 3), EIN letter (Step 4), signed Operating Agreement (Step 5), government-issued ID for all signatories.

**Documents typically required:**
- Stamped Certificate of Formation
- IRS EIN confirmation letter (CP 575)
- Signed Operating Agreement
- Government-issued photo ID for all authorized signatories
- Initial deposit (varies by bank — as low as $0 at some banks, $1,000+ at others)

**Recommended banks for small fund managers:**
- **Mercury** (mercury.com): Online business banking, zero fees, fast account opening, API access. Popular with fintech-adjacent funds.
- **SVB / First Citizens** (post-SVB acquisition): Traditional relationship banking for fund managers. Higher minimums.
- **JPMorgan Chase Business:** Large bank, in-person opening, good for eventual prime brokerage relationships.

**Note:** Keep management company operating account strictly separate from fund account. Fund assets must be held in the fund's own accounts, not the manager's.

**Estimated cost:** $0–$1,000 initial deposit (varies)
**Estimated time:** 1–5 business days

**When done, you have:** An active business checking account for the management company.

## Step 7 — File Beneficial Ownership Information Report with FinCEN

- [ ] **Status:** Not started / In progress / Complete

**What you need before starting:** Certificate of Formation (Step 3), government-issued ID for all beneficial owners.

**What this is:** The Corporate Transparency Act (CTA), effective January 1, 2024, requires most U.S. LLCs to report beneficial ownership information (BOI) to the Financial Crimes Enforcement Network (FinCEN).

**Deadline:**
- LLCs formed before January 1, 2024: initial filing deadline was January 1, 2025.
- LLCs formed on or after January 1, 2024: **within 90 days of formation**.
- LLCs formed on or after January 1, 2025: **within 30 days of formation**.

**Who must be reported:** Any individual who (1) owns or controls 25%+ of the LLC, or (2) exercises substantial control over the LLC (including senior officers and decision-makers). Most fund management company founders will report themselves.

**Required fields per beneficial owner:**
- Full legal name
- Date of birth
- Current residential address
- Unique identifying number (driver's license, passport, or FinCEN identifier)
- Copy of the identifying document

**How to file:** FinCEN BOI E-Filing System at boiefiling.fincen.gov. Filing is free.

**Penalties for non-filing:** Up to $591/day in civil penalties and up to $10,000 in criminal penalties. This is not optional.

**Estimated cost:** $0
**Estimated time:** 30 minutes

**When done, you have:** FinCEN BOI confirmation — retain this receipt.

## Step 8 — Register for State Taxes If Applicable

- [ ] **Status:** Not started / In progress / Complete

**What you need before starting:** EIN (Step 4), knowledge of where the LLC operates and where employees (if any) will work.

**What you do:**
- Delaware gross receipts tax: If you do business in Delaware, you may owe Delaware gross receipts tax. Most fund managers operating outside Delaware are not subject to this.
- **Home state registration:** If you operate in a state other than Delaware (which most managers do), you may need to register as a "foreign LLC" in your home state. This typically requires filing a Foreign Qualification form and paying a registration fee.
- **State income tax registration:** If you have employees in your home state, register for state income tax withholding.
- **State business license:** Some states and cities require a general business license. Check with your home state and municipality.

**Common home state foreign qualification fees:**
- California: $70 + $800 minimum franchise tax
- New York: $250
- Texas: $750
- Florida: $125

**Estimated cost:** $0–$800 (varies significantly by state)
**Estimated time:** 1–2 weeks

**When done, you have:** Foreign LLC qualification in your operating state (if required) and any required state tax registrations.

---

## Step 9 — File NFA CPO Exemption 4.13(a)(3) If Applicable

- [ ] **Status:** Not started / In progress / Complete

**What you need before starting:** EIN (Step 4), entity formed, understanding of your fund's trading strategy.

**Who needs this:** Any fund manager whose fund trades commodity interests (futures, swaps, options on futures) must either register as a Commodity Pool Operator (CPO) or claim an exemption. Exemption 4.13(a)(3) is the most common for small systematic funds.

**See the full guide:** [NFA CPO Exemption Guide](nfa-cpo-exemption-guide.md)

**At a minimum, know before proceeding:**
- You must be a member of NFA to claim the exemption (NFA membership fee: ~$750 for first year, $200/year after).
- You must file the exemption claim on NFA's BASIC system.
- The exemption requires annual affirmation.
- General solicitation is prohibited under 4.13(a)(3).

**NFA BASIC system:** nfa.futures.org/BasicNet/

**Estimated cost:** $750–$2,500 NFA membership
**Estimated time:** 1–2 weeks including NFA approval

**When done, you have:** NFA membership confirmation and 4.13(a)(3) exemption notice on BASIC.

---

## Step 10 — Obtain Required State Business Licenses

- [ ] **Status:** Not started / In progress / Complete

**What you need before starting:** Entity formed, home state foreign qualification complete (Step 8), clarity on services offered.

**What you do:**
- Check your home state's investment adviser registration requirements. If you are an investment adviser (advising on securities), you may need to register with your state securities regulator. See the [IA Registration Guide](../regulatory/ia-registration-guide.md).
- Check for local (city/county) business licenses. Many municipalities require a general business license regardless of entity type.
- Check for any state-specific financial services licenses. New York, for example, has additional requirements.

**Resources:**
- SBA.gov business license search by state
- State securities regulator directory: NASAA.org
- Your state's Secretary of State business license portal

**Estimated cost:** $0–$500 (varies by jurisdiction)
**Estimated time:** 1–4 weeks

**When done, you have:** All required state and local licenses in hand. The management company is operational.
